## KEY CHANGE: 2002 → 2023

NFPA 70B changed from a **"Recommended Practice"** (advisory) to a **"Standard"** (mandatory "shall" language) in the 2023 edition. This is a fundamental shift — exam questions may test this distinction.

## EXTRACTION PRIORITY TIERS

### TIER 1 — CRITICAL (Copy Every Word + All Tables)

These chapters directly map to the highest-weighted NETA ETT exam domains (Component Testing 35-48%, Electrical Theory 22-25%).

---

#### Chapter 4 — General
**Copy sections:** 4.1 through 4.8* (all)
**Why:** Defines EMP (Electrical Maintenance Program) fundamentals, personnel qualifications, survey/analysis requirements, planned inspections, acceptance test reports. Replaces old EPM concepts. Maps to Level III/IV program management KSAs.
**NETA KSAs:** III.A.1, IV.A.1, IV.A.2
**Include:** All tables in this chapter

#### Chapter 7 — Fundamental Tests
**Copy sections:** 7.1 through 7.4 (all)
**Why:** Core testing content — bolted bus connections, conductor terminations, insulation resistance quality, infrared thermography. This is the testing foundation for the entire standard.
**NETA KSAs:** II.B.1, II.D.2, II.E.1, III.B.1, ET-016 through ET-019
**Include:** ALL tables from Chapter 7 (linked as "Tables From Chapter 7")

#### Chapter 8 — Field Testing and Test Methods
**Copy sections:** 8.1 through 8.7* (all)
**Why:** Testing categories (acceptance/maintenance/special), test personnel qualifications, test equipment, test records, condition of maintenance indication. Highest exam relevance for testing methodology.
**NETA KSAs:** II.B.1, III.B.1, IV.B.1, CT-all categories
**Include:** ALL tables

#### Chapter 9 — Maintenance Intervals
**Copy sections:** 9.1 through 9.3 (all)
**Why:** Frequency of maintenance tables, equipment condition assessment methodology. Directly tested on exam — "how often" and "what triggers" maintenance.
**NETA KSAs:** III.A.1, IV.A.1, IV.B.1
**Include:** ALL tables from Chapter 9 (these are exam gold)

#### Chapter 11 — Power and Distribution Transformers
**Copy sections:** 11.1 through 11.3 (all)
**Why:** Transformer maintenance is 15-20% of Component Testing domain. Procedures for liquid-filled and dry-type, DGA, oil analysis, insulation testing.
**NETA KSAs:** CT-005:009, II.D.1, II.D.2, III.B.2
**Include:** ALL tables from Chapter 11

#### Chapter 12 — Substations and Switchgear
**Copy sections:** 12.1 through 12.3 (all)
**Why:** Switchgear maintenance is a core exam topic — visual inspection, cleaning, insulation testing, bus connections, interlocks.
**NETA KSAs:** CT-001:004, III.B.3
**Include:** ALL tables from Chapter 12

#### Chapter 15 — Circuit Breakers, Low- and Medium-Voltage
**Copy sections:** 15.1 through 15.4 (all)
**Why:** Breaker testing is heavily exam-weighted. Covers LVPCB, MCCB, ICCB, and MV power circuit breakers. Trip testing, contact resistance, timing, insulation resistance.
**NETA KSAs:** CT-024:027, III.C.1
**Include:** ALL tables from Chapter 15

#### Chapter 18 — Power Cables and Conductors
**Copy sections:** 18.1* through 18.3 (all)
**Why:** Cable testing (IR, hi-pot, VLF, PD, TDR) is a major exam domain. Maintenance procedures and acceptance criteria.
**NETA KSAs:** CT-010:015, III.B.4
**Include:** ALL tables from Chapter 18

#### Chapter 20 — Grounding and Bonding
**Copy sections:** 20.1 through 20.3 (all)
**Why:** Ground testing (fall-of-potential, continuity, impedance) appears at all exam levels. Maintenance procedures for grounding systems.
**NETA KSAs:** CT-047:056
**Include:** ALL tables from Chapter 20

#### Chapter 27 — Rotating Equipment
**Copy sections:** 27.1* through 27.3 (all)
**Why:** Motor/generator testing — insulation resistance, PI, winding resistance, vibration, surge comparison. Multi-level exam topic.
**NETA KSAs:** CT-055:064
**Include:** ALL tables from Chapter 27

#### Chapter 35 — Protective Relays
**Copy sections:** 35.1 through 35.3 (all)
**Why:** Relay maintenance/testing is a significant exam topic — pickup, timing, instantaneous, trip testing.
**NETA KSAs:** CT-033:039, III.C.1
**Include:** ALL tables from Chapter 35

#### Chapter 36 — Stationary Standby Batteries
**Copy sections:** 36.1* through 36.5 (all)
**Why:** Battery testing (capacity, impedance, conductance, specific gravity) is tested at all levels. Includes documentation and special procedures.
**NETA KSAs:** CT-064:076
**Include:** ALL tables from Chapter 36

## COPY-PASTE WORKFLOW

### Step 1: Start with Tier 1 chapters (12 chapters)
Open each chapter on NFPA Link. Select all text from the chapter heading through the last table. Paste into a single markdown file per chapter or one consolidated file.

### Step 2: Include ALL tables
Tables are the most exam-relevant content. On NFPA Link, tables are sometimes linked separately ("Tables From Chapter X"). **Click into each table link and copy separately.**

### Step 3: Paste format
For each chapter, create a text block like:

```
=== CHAPTER [N] — [Title] ===

[Paste all chapter text here]

=== TABLES FROM CHAPTER [N] ===

[Paste all table content here]
```

### Step 4: Consolidation options
Either:
- **Single file:** `NFPA-70B-2023-Full-Extraction-Raw.md` (all chapters in one file)
- **Per-tier files:** `NFPA-70B-2023-Tier1-Raw.md`, `NFPA-70B-2023-Tier2-Raw.md`, etc.

### Step 5: Hand off for extraction processing
Once raw text is pasted, I will process it into the standard extraction format with KSA tags, replacing the outdated 2002 extraction.
