## What This Means

### The Stakes
| Dimension | Reality |
|-----------|---------|
| Personal Investment | 3-4 years of time + personal money |
| Current Pain | Cannot effectively manage workload |
| Who's Affected | Jason, his boss, the field technicians |
| Competition | Failed D365 demo from corporate |
| Opportunity | PE ownership = potential buyout/adoption |
| Success Metric | "Employee of the year" - but really: operational survival |

### The Problem Space
- **Disconnected systems** - Nothing talks to each other
- **Canned solutions** - Don't fit actual workflows
- **Zero visibility** - Operations team can't see anything
- **Redundant effort** - Same data entered multiple places
- **Outdated thinking** - Pre-API/integration era mindset

### The Solution Requirements
1. **Centralized visibility** - One place to see everything
2. **Modern architecture** - APIs, integrations, real-time
3. **Cohesive data** - Estimates → Projects → Scheduling → Completion
4. **Quality + Efficiency + Consistency** - The three pillars

## Strategic Implications for Both Claudes

### What NOT to Do
- Don't build features nobody asked for
- Don't over-engineer for hypothetical scale
- Don't lose sight of the core value: **VISIBILITY**

### What TO Do
- Build the visibility layer FIRST
- Make it clean, modern, obvious
- Connect the data that's currently siloed
- Prove value to Jason's region, then expand

### The Real MVP
Not a checklist app. Not a scheduling replacement.

**The MVP is:** A dashboard that shows Jason and his boss, in one glance:
- Where are all our projects?
- What's the status of each?
- Who's working on what?
- What needs attention right now?

If we build that, everything else follows.
